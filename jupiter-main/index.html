TOUR TO JUPITER

Jupiter is the largest planet in the solar system, so there is nothing strange about this planet symbolizing power in astrology.

Due to its strong gravity, Jupiter acts as a kind of shield for our Earth, attracting camets and asteroids to itself. Gravitational forces most likely participated in the final formation of our system and, possibly, even allowed life to appear on Earth itself.

The planet is so huge that it would take over 1,300 Earth-sized planets to fill the volume of this gas giant.

Tour information:

A day on Jupiter lasts 10 Earth hours, and a year lasts 12 Earth years, so you will come back to Earth as young and energetic. years.

Humanity still does not know of any planets that would exceed Jupiter in size. Astrologers consider this giant to be the king of all planets. And the atmosphere of Jupiter is more like the atmosphere of a star than a planet. And it sounds even more interesting: visit a star!

In addition to the fact that it very often rains on Jupiter from real diamonds. Another attraction of this gas giant is the Great Red Spot.

This spot is a giant hurricane, which is constantly changing in size, then changing its brightness and color. You can have a really bright photo session!

The gas giant rotates on its axis faster than any other planet in the solar system. Jupiter has a large satellite family, often referred to as the Miniature Solar System.

The planet also makes sounds similar to human speech. They are also called "electromagnetic voices". You will definitely not be bored here!

Way

According to popular misconception, Jupiter is a "failed star." But this is not the case. To become at least a brown dwarf, the gas giant would have to weigh about 12.5 times more than it is now, and for constant hydrogen-based fusion reactions to start inside Jupiter, it needs to be about 80 times more massive.
