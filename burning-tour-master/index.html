Interesting Sun Facts:

If we imagine the Sun in the form of a hollow sphere, then more than a million planets the size of our Earth could fit in it.
Using computer models, scientists have concluded that the sun is approximately 4.5 billion years old. It is believed that a star as massive as the Sun should have existed for about 10 billion years before transitioning to the red giant stage.
When the sun begins to die, it will grow to such a size that it engulfs Mercury, Venus, and probably the Earth.
Only 40% of the solar radiation reaches the Earth, while 60% of the radiation is reflected and goes back into space.
The color of human skin in different regions of our planet depends on the angle at which the Sun stands above the horizon in the middle of the day. This is due to the fact that the angle of the Sun affects the adaptation of the organism to external conditions in the process of evolution.
